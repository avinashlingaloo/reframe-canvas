About This Method

MITRE's Look Inward / Look Outward / Reframe helps you avoid jumping to solutions by first examining what you know, then what's happening externally, before redefining the problem itself.

◐
Look Inward
Examine assumptions, capabilities, constraints
◑
Look Outward
Scan trends, analogies, external perspectives
●
Reframe
Synthesize insights to redefine the problem
Phase 1 of 3

Look Inward

Examine your own capabilities, assumptions, and constraints.

◐
Assumptions & Biases

◐
Capabilities & Strengths

◐
Constraints & Gaps

Who Experiences It?
Who is affected? How do their lived experiences vary?
Phase 2 of 3

Look Outward

Scan the external environment for trends, analogies, and stakeholder perspectives.

◑
Who Else Has It?
How do colleagues, competitors, or other domains deal with similar problems?
◑
Who Does Not Have It?
Was it avoided, mitigated, solved, or transferred? Why?
◑
Who Has Been Left Out?
Whose perspective is missing? Let's broaden our view.
◑
Who Benefits?
Who benefits when this problem exists? When it doesn't?
