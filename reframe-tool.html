About This Method

MITRE's Look Inward / Look Outward / Reframe is a structured approach to strategic problem-solving. It helps you avoid jumping to solutions by first thoroughly examining what you know, then what's happening externally, before redefining the problem itself.

Phase 1 of 3

Look Inward

Examine your own organization's capabilities, assumptions, and constraints. What do you already know? What biases might be shaping how you see this problem?

◐
Assumptions
What beliefs or assumptions are you (or your team) holding about this problem? Which ones have you never questioned?
◐
Capabilities & Strengths
What resources, skills, or advantages does your organization have that are relevant here?
◐
Constraints & Gaps
What limitations, resource gaps, or organizational barriers exist? What have you tried before that didn't work?
◐
Blind Spots
What might you be overlooking? Whose perspective is missing? What data don't you have?
Phase 2 of 3

Look Outward

Scan the external environment. What's happening in adjacent domains? What emerging trends, competitor moves, or stakeholder needs might reshape how you think about this?

◑
Trends & Signals
What emerging trends in technology, industry, or society are relevant to this challenge?
◑
Analogous Solutions
How have other industries or domains solved similar problems? What can you learn from unrelated fields?
◑
Stakeholder Perspectives
What do your customers, partners, or end-users actually need? How might their needs differ from what you've assumed?
◑
Competitive Landscape
What are competitors or adjacent players doing? Where is the gap between what exists and what's possible?
Phase 3 of 3 — Synthesis

Reframe

Now bring it all together. Review what you've captured, look for patterns and tensions, and redefine the problem in a way that opens new solution spaces.

The Reframed Problem

Based on everything you've explored, restate the challenge. This should feel meaningfully different from your original problem statement.
